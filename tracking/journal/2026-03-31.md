### Lessons Learned

1. **Price slippage is the dominant cost, not fees.** Entry price gap between km and flx legs (~$0.09-$0.15/share) creates uPnL drag much larger than the extremely low trading fees ($0.74 roundtrip).

2. **Funding must be large enough to cover slippage.** TSLA earned $6.07 funding → covered $2.10 slippage + $0.74 fees → profitable. NVDA earned only $0.68 → couldn't cover $4.07 slippage → loss.

3. **Symbol selection matters more than venue selection.** Both pairs used the same venues (km+flx) with the same low fees, but TSLA's funding differential was ~9x larger than NVDA's.

4. **Entry execution quality is critical.** Tighter entry execution (minimizing price gap between long and short fills) would directly improve P&L. Consider:
   - Limit orders instead of market
   - Simultaneous execution (reduce time gap between legs)
   - Smaller fills to reduce market impact

5. **NVDA should have been skipped.** From the pre-trade analysis, NVDA kinetiq/felix spread was ~36% APR weekend but actual funding realized was only $0.68 — far below projected. Need to validate funding rate DURING weekend, not just historical average.

### Strategy Viability Assessment

- **Fees**: Extremely competitive at 0.0074% avg — NOT a bottleneck
- **Slippage**: The real cost — ~$2-4 per $2.5k leg per weekend
- **Funding**: Must be >$3/leg/weekend (>~15% APR spread) to be consistently profitable after slippage
- **Verdict**: Strategy viable for high-funding-spread symbols (TSLA worked). Not viable for low-spread symbols (NVDA failed). Need stricter entry criteria.
